# How It Works

Every engagement follows three phases - Acquire, Build and Educate, and Retain and Expand. The process is designed so your team learns while the systems get built, not after.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acquire - discovery and strategy">
    We start with a discovery call to understand your business, operations, and goals. We identify the highest-ROI opportunities for AI in your workflows. You receive a strategy proposal with specific recommendations, timelines, and expected returns. You decide whether to move forward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and Educate - weeks 0 through 8">
    This is where the work happens, and it happens simultaneously.

    **Week 0:** Kickoff call. Your assigned engineer meets your team, maps your workflows, and sets up infrastructure. Education planning begins on day one.

    **Weeks 1-3:** Sprint builds start delivering working systems every one to two weeks. Education sessions run in parallel - your team learns while watching the systems come to life.

    **Weeks 4-8:** Full deployment. Systems go live in production. Team training continues until your people can operate and extend everything independently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retain and Expand - week 9 onward">
    The engagement does not end at deployment. We provide ongoing advisory, monitor system performance, and build new features as your needs evolve. Monthly strategy reviews help you discover new opportunities as your team's AI capabilities mature. Most clients expand their scope as they see what is possible.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  Build and Educate are not sequential phases. Education runs in parallel from day one. Your team learns while the systems get built - not after.
</Callout>

## Why this works

The simultaneous approach creates a compounding effect. As your team learns, they identify new opportunities. As systems get deployed, they become teaching tools. The build reveals what to learn next. The education reveals what to build next.

This is what makes the relationship defensible and the value sustainable. You are not just getting software - you are building organizational capability.
