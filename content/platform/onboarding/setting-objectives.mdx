# Setting Objectives

Step two of the onboarding wizard asks you to set your first business objective. Objectives are the foundation of your Advizr engagement - they shape the workflows your team builds, the recommendations you receive, and how progress is measured. This page explains how to write a strong objective and why it matters.

## Why objectives matter

Your Advizr team uses your objectives to decide what to build first, which documents to prioritise for analysis, and what learning content to recommend. Without a clear objective, the team has to guess at what matters most to you. A well-defined objective saves time for everyone and leads to better results faster.

Objectives also appear on your Goals page, where you can track progress over time and see how the work being done connects to your business outcomes.

## How to write a good objective

The best objectives are specific and measurable. They answer two questions: what do you want to change, and how will you know it worked?

<Callout type="tip" title="Examples of strong objectives">
  - "Reduce manual data entry in our invoicing process by 50% within three months."
  - "Automate our monthly financial reporting so it takes hours instead of days."
  - "Generate qualified leads from our website without adding headcount."
  - "Create a standard onboarding process for new hires that runs without manual chasing."
</Callout>

Compare those with vague objectives like "use AI more" or "improve efficiency." The specific versions tell your Advizr team exactly where to focus.

## Setting your objective in the wizard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the prompt">
    The wizard asks you what you want to achieve with AI in your business. Read the prompt and think about the single most important area where AI could save you time or money.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your objective">
    Type your objective in the text field. Be as specific as you can. Include numbers, timeframes, or outcomes if possible.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and continue">
    Review what you have written and click Continue. Your objective is saved to your Goals page and shared with your Advizr team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Updating objectives later

You are not locked into your first objective. You can add new objectives, edit existing ones, or close completed ones from the [Goals](/docs/platform/dashboard/goals) section at any time. Most clients add two or three more objectives after their kickoff call.
