# Analytics

The Analytics section shows you how your Advizr engagement is performing. It brings together workflow metrics, goal progress, and usage data into a single dashboard. Use it to understand what is working, measure the return on your investment, and identify where to focus next.

## What you can see

The analytics dashboard covers three main areas:

### Workflow performance

See how your automated workflows are performing over time. Key metrics include:

- **Run count** - How many times each workflow has executed
- **Success rate** - The percentage of runs that completed without errors
- **Processing time** - How long each run takes on average
- **Output volume** - How much work each workflow is producing

### Goal progress

Track your business objectives alongside the work being done. Each goal shows its current completion percentage and the workflows contributing to it. This connects the day-to-day automation work to your bigger-picture objectives.

### Usage metrics

Understand how you and your team are using the platform:

- Chat conversations started and messages sent
- Documents uploaded and processed
- Proposals reviewed and approved
- Learning content completed

<Callout type="info">
  Analytics data is specific to your organisation. It reflects only your workflows, your goals, and your team's activity.
</Callout>

## Reading the dashboard

The dashboard defaults to a 30-day view. Use the date picker to change the time range. Charts update automatically to reflect the selected period.

Key numbers are displayed at the top as summary cards - total runs, hours saved, active goals, and team members. Below the summary cards, detailed charts break down performance by workflow and by time period.

## Exporting reports

You can export analytics data for use in your own reporting. Click **Export** to download a summary as CSV or PDF. Exports include the current date range and all visible metrics.

## How analytics inform next steps

Your FDE reviews your analytics regularly and uses the data to recommend adjustments. If a workflow is underperforming, they will investigate and propose changes. If a goal is progressing faster than expected, they may suggest expanding scope or adding new objectives.
