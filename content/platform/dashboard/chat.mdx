# Chat

Chat is the central interface for working with Advizr. It is where you ask questions, request deliverables, analyse documents, get status updates, and provide feedback. Every conversation is saved, searchable, and tied to your business context. If you are new to Chat, start with the [Using Chat](/docs/platform/onboarding/using-chat) onboarding guide.

## The three-pane layout

On desktop, the chat screen is split into three resizable panels:

### Threads (left panel)

Every conversation lives in a thread. Old threads are listed here with a title and timestamp. Click a thread to resume it. Click **New Thread** at the top to start a fresh conversation. You can search through past threads using the search bar.

### Conversation (centre panel)

This is the active chat. Your messages appear on the right; AI responses appear on the left. Type your message in the input box at the bottom and press Enter to send. You can also attach files by clicking the attachment icon or dragging a file into the conversation.

### Context (right panel)

The context panel shows information about the current conversation:

- **Active agent** - Which AI agent is handling your request
- **Referenced documents** - Files the AI used to inform its response
- **Tools** - Any tools or workflows being invoked during the conversation

<Callout type="info">
  Chat is your primary way to work with the Advizr platform. Most tasks can be started, tracked, and completed entirely within Chat.
</Callout>

## Message types

You can send several types of content in Chat:

- **Text messages** - Questions, instructions, or feedback in plain language
- **File attachments** - Upload a document directly into the conversation for immediate analysis
- **Links** - Share URLs for the AI or your team to review

The AI may respond with formatted text, bullet lists, tables, document references, or suggested next actions.

## Mobile experience

On screens smaller than 768px, the three-pane layout collapses to a single column. The thread list and context panel become slide-over drawers accessible from the top bar. The conversation remains the primary view.

## Conversation history

All conversations are saved automatically. You can return to any past thread at any time from the threads panel. Use the search bar to find conversations by keyword. There is no limit on the number of threads you can create.

<Callout type="tip">
  Start a new thread for each distinct topic or request. This keeps your conversation history organised and makes it easier to find past discussions.
</Callout>

## When AI connects you with your team

Some requests are better handled by a person. When the AI determines that your question needs human input - a strategic decision, a pricing discussion, or a complex technical question - it flags the conversation for your FDE. You will see a note in the chat when this happens, and your FDE will follow up.
